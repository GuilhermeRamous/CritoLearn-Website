History of Bitcoin

Before we go into the history of Bitcoin it is necessary to have a basic understanding of its functioning, but do not worry if you do not understand now, beacause I will write a complete article about it.

Bitcoin is basically a crypto-currency, money that uses encryption to control its creation and transactions, which runs through a decentralized point-to-point network where only users are required to manage the system, having no central intermediary or authority.

Bitcoins transactions are made from one virtual wallet to the other, where one sends the crypto-coins and another receives. Such transactions are processed by several computers of great computational power, called miners. They are then recorded in the blockchain, a sort of public and immutable book that ensures no transaction will be altered. Each transaction is calculated a rate that is given to the miners.

Bitcoin origins

Many people think that the concept of criptocurrency only came into existence with the creation of bitcoin, but they are wrong, the concept was introduced by the user Wei Dai on a cypherpunk mailing list. Examples of pre-Bitcoin projects are B-money (idealized by Wei Dai) and Bit Gold, though they were not fully developed as Bitcoin.

In 2008, a paper called Bitcoin - A Peer to Peer Electronic Cash System, which featured Bitcoin and explained its operation, was published on a mailing list. The paper was published by Satoshi Nakamoto, creator of Bitcoin, whose identity remains a mystery.

In 2009, Nakamoto released the first software for bitcoins mining and partnered with developers to continue developing and improving bitcoin. Just a year later, bitcoin was used as a form of payment for the first time, to buy a pizza. All of this happened in Bitcoin Talk, a forum created by Nakamoto to discuss about cryptocurrencies, where a user offered 10,000 BTCs to anyone who buys a pizza using Bitcoin and delivers to their home. Today 10,000 BTC is worth $ 245631210.00 (verified on 08/08/2018).

Satoshi Nakamoto left the project:

Gradually lefting Bitcoin Talk and stopped exchanging emails with the developers of the project, until in 2011 he left the project. However, according to a community-accepted suspicion, Nakamoto did not come out of empty pockets, pocketing 980,000 BTC, equivalent to R $ 23,723,110,409.60 (verified on 08/13/2018).

Many people question whether Nakamoto's departure from the project was a good choice, imagining the consequences of having a central figure in a technology that has the power to change the course of life for all of us.

New Cryptocurrencies arise:

Com o crescimento da Bitcoin e da ideia de haver moedas descentralizadas e criptografadas, novas criptomoedas surgiram.

Normalmente, elas surgiam se baseando no código-fonte da Bitcoin, mas trazendo algumas melhorias e ideias que na Bitcoin não havia. Hoje há mais de 1 milhão de criptomoedas, todas criadas a partir de 2011, com exceção da Bitcoin.

Roubo

Entretanto, a mesma arquitetura que com anonimidade permite liberdade, também permite roubo sem punição.

Em janeiro de 2014, Mt.Gox, a maior casa de câmbio de Bitcoin foi hackeada, sendo roubada 850.000 BTC. Até hoje, investigadores tentam entender como o roubo foi feito e quem foi que o fez.

O Futuro da Bitcoin

Com o aumento de lugares onde se pode usar Bitcoin, a popularidade da moeda cresceu em um período em que o valor permaneceu abaixo dos picos anteriores. Com tudo isso, ficou evidente que o dinheiro estava fluindo para ecossistema da Bitcoin e de outras moedas. Bancos como Barclays, o Citi Bank, o Deutsche Bank e o BNP Paribas dizem estar investigando formas de trabalhar com Bitcoin, e enqunto isso a blockchain, tecnologia por trás da Bitcoin, vem revolucionando a industria financeira, além de outras áreas.

Seja você um ávido adorador da Bitcoin ou um cético, é inegável que a Bitcoin e as outras criptomoedas vieram para ficar, agora se trará tudo aquilo que promete só o tempo dirá.
